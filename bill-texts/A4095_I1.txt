SYNOPSIS 
 
Allows county sheriffs to issue firearms identification cards and permits to 
purchase handguns; authorizes county sheriffs to accept applications for 
permits to carry handguns.

STATEMENT 
32 
 
33 
 
This bill provides county sheriffs with the authority to issue 
34 
firearms purchaser identification cards and permits to purchase 
35 
handguns, and provides them with authority to accept applications 
36 
for permits to carry handguns. 
37 
 
Under current law, a State resident applying for a firearms 
38 
purchaser identification card or a permit to purchase a handgun 
39 
must make such application to the chief law enforcement officer of 
40 
an organized full-time police department of the municipality where 
41 
the applicant resides or the Superintendent of State Police if the 
42 
municipality does not have a full-time police force.  Out-of-State 
43 
residents must make application to the superintendent.  The chief 
44 
law enforcement officer, or superintendent, as the case may be, is 
45 
tasked with taking fingerprints and initiating a criminal history 
46 
records check on the applicant.  Upon completion of the 
47 
background investigation, the applicant is issued the identification 
48 


=== Page 19 ===
 
A4095 WEBBER, FANTASIA 
19 
 
 
card or permit by the chief law enforcement officer or 
1 
superintendent.  Under the provisions of this bill, county sheriffs 
2 
are granted the same authority for State residents. 
3 
 
In the instance of an application for a permit to carry a handgun, 
4 
the initial application process is similar under current law.  An 
5 
applicant must make application to the chief law enforcement 
6 
officer of an organized full-time police department of the 
7 
municipality where the applicant resides or the Superintendent of 
8 
State Police if the municipality does not have a full-time police 
9 
force.  Out-of-State residents and employees of armored trucks must 
10 
make application to the superintendent.  Upon approval of the 
11 
application by the chief law enforcement officer, or superintendent, 
12 
the Superior Court issues the permit.  The bill provides county 
13 
sheriffs with the authority to accept, investigate and approve or 
14 
deny permit to carry applications for State residents.
